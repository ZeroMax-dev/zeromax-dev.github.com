Touch-Optimized Explorer Design

Description:

This work is an interface study that was developed at the University of Arts Bremen.

Especially now that market shares of tablet computers and other mobile touch devices gradually surpass that of conventional personal computers it is important to adapt established interface concepts to these new interaction paradigms. Until now prevailing mobile systems have failed to provide a touch-oriented filesystem explorer that features all the capabilities known from desktop systems in an efficient manner. The interface mockup presented here aims to provide a solution to this shortcoming.

In this design concept, however, not only adaptions were performed. Established concepts that we use in our everyday work may be effective but not necessarily efficient. Concepts like multiple tabs have been around in web browsers for a while now but on most systems still haven't been applied to their filesystem explorers yet. Together with the simplification of UI functions and iconography the concept aims for an intuitive and drastically improved workflow, yet providing a high degree of flexibilty for larger and more complex tasks.
